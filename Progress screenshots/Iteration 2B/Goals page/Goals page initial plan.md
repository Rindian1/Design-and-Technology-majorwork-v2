 Add GOALS feature: 
    -Here the app challenges the user to make certain changes to their energy usage, in small specific ways.  
        -e.g. Shift your energy usage forward by one hour 
        -  Reduce energy usage by 5% today  
        - Keep up your X day streak of below 10% of your budget
        -  e.t.c. 
    The user can then choose specific goals to target. By the end of the day, or week, the user can then see if they achieved their goals, and if not see their progress. This allows for the app to reinforce positive habits. 


Key features:
User Autonomy & Opt-In Selection (The Activation Circle):
Rather than forcing users to try to complete rigid targets, users have full agency to activate challenges that suit their lifestyle and circumstances. This allows for the app to hold users accountable but not create stress with unrealistic expectations, as the user can choose whether they want to activate a challenge.

Progress markers for goals: 
Goals can be either linear or 'checkpoint'/streak based. For each these two types of goals, different progress markers are used:
- Segmented Progress Bars are used for tracking streak-based challenges (e.g., 3/5 days or 1/3 days), giving immediate visual confirmation of consecutive behavioral habits.
- Linear Progress Bars are used for a linear percentage-based goal (e.g., 20% used out of a 100% budget limit), allowing users to monitor their buffer thresholds in real-time.

State Contrast visually distinguishes between active, colored goals and the low-opacity, greyed-out inactive goal ("Spend 10% less energy..."), decluttering the user's cognitive load.

Ethical Gamification (The "Stake" Mechanic):
To drive behavioral compliance without introducing toxic stress or financial anxiety, the application utilizes a "Stake" system rather than a direct failure penalty.

The Reward: Completing a goal awards the user with a specified points yield (e.g., 200 points), which can be cumulative or scaled daily.

The Risk Mitigation (Stake): Upon activating a challenge, the user "stakes" a portion of their potential earnings (e.g., -100 points). If they fail to meet the goal, they forfeit this stake. By framing this as an opt-in risk rather than a top-down penalty, the system leverages loss aversion (behavioral economics) to motivate action, while ethically ensuring that a user's point baseline is never aggressively damaged by factors out of their control (e.g., unexpected weather events).
